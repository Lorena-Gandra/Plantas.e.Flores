    Why have plants at home?

    1-Feed the world – All the food we consume comes from plants. Even the livestock we eat, in turn depends on plants for food. In other words, plants are the basis of almost all food webs, or what ecologists call a trophic level.

    2-Understand fundamental processes – Plants are convenient organisms in which fundamental processes (such as cell division and protein synthesis, for example) can be studied, without the ethical dilemma of these studies in animals or humans. The laws of genetic inheritance were discovered in this way by Gregor Mendel who was studying the way in which the shape of peas was inherited.

    3-Using medicines and materials – Many drugs, medicinal or not, come from the Vegetal Kingdom. Aspirin, originally extracted from willow bark, is an example. The cure for several diseases can come from vegetables, waiting to be discovered. Popular stimulants such as coffee, chocolate, tobacco, and tea also originate from plants. Most alcoholic beverages are obtained from the fermentation of plants, such as hops and grapes. Plants provide us with many natural materials: cotton, wood, paper, linen, vegetable oils, some types of rope and rubber are some examples.
